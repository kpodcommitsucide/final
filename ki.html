Kommissbrot (German Military Rye Bread)

Kommissbrot was a dense, long-lasting rye bread used by the German military from the 19th century through WWII. It was designed to be nutritious, cheap, and durable, making it a staple for soldiers.

Ingredients:

  • 500g (4 cups) rye flour
  • 250g (2 cups) whole wheat flour
  • 10g (2 tsp) salt
  • 7g (1 packet) dry yeast or 20g fresh yeast
  • 450ml (1 Â¾ cups) lukewarm water
  • 1 tsp sugar or honey (to help yeast)
  • 1 tbsp vinegar (optional, for acidity and preservation)

Instructions:

  1. Mix yeast, sugar, and lukewarm water. Let sit for 5-10 minutes until foamy.
  2. In a large bowl, combine rye flour, whole wheat flour, and salt. Add yeast mixture and vinegar. Mix until a sticky dough forms.
  3. Knead for about 10 minutes until smooth. Cover and let rise for 2 hours in a warm place.
  4. Shape into a loaf and place in a greased tin. Let rise again for 30-45 minutes.
  5. Simmer for about 45 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Preheat oven to 200Â°C (400Â°F). Bake for 50-60 minutes until the crust is firm and the loaf sounds hollow when tapped.
  7. Let cool completely. Traditionally, this bread was stored for days and eaten in slices with butter, jam, or sausage.
