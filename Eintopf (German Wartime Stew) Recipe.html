Eintopf (German Wartime Stew) Recipe

This was a common meal in Nazi Germany, promoted as an affordable, nutritious dish to unify the nation.

Ingredients:

  • 500g (1 lb) potatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 200g (7 oz) cabbage, shredded
  • 100g (3.5 oz) sausage or meat substitute (rationing made meat rare)
  • 1 liter (4 cups) water or broth
  • 1 tbsp lard or oil (or margarine, which was more common)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the lard or oil in a large pot.
  2. Add the onions and cook until translucent.
  3. Add the carrots, potatoes, and cabbage, stirring well.
  4. Pour in the water or broth, bring to a boil, then reduce heat.
  5. Simmer for about 45 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. If available, add sliced sausage or a small amount of meat.
  7. Season with salt and pepper. Serve hot.
